🚀 What is HydraDB?

✅ Easy to grasp

HydraDB is a memory system for AI apps. It stores information about users and lets your AI recall the right things at the right time.

You can use it for individual users (B2C) or for whole organisations with many teams (B2B).

💾 Memories

✅ Easy to grasp

A memory is any piece of information your AI needs to remember — emails, messages, documents, preferences, images, web pages, and more.

HydraDB breaks each memory down carefully so your AI can find and use it as accurately as possible.

🔁 Recall — The Hard Part

⚠️ You struggled here

Storing memories is easy. Knowing which memory to fetch, when, and why — that's the hard part.

When your AI asks HydraDB for something, it goes through several steps:

  • Understands what the AI is trying to do
  • Considers who is asking and in what context
  • Follows relationships between concepts (graph traversal)
  • Weighs how recent, relevant, and frequently used each memory is
  • Returns the most useful result — not just the most similar one
HydraDB tip: Think of it as the difference between Google Search and a personal librarian who knows your entire reading history.

⚡ What Makes HydraDB Different

⚠️ You struggled here

Normal AI memory treats everything like a flat list. HydraDB treats memory like a living graph — concepts are connected, and those connections matter.

Every recall considers: who is asking, which agent, what they're trying to do, and what worked before.

🎯 Adaptive & Personalised Retrieval

⚠️ You struggled here

Over time, HydraDB learns which memories actually help each user:

  • Two people asking the same question may get different answers
  • Frequently used memories are ranked higher automatically
  • Useless memories get quietly deprioritised
💡 Like Spotify's algorithm — but for your AI's memory. It learns what to surface and what to skip.

🔍 Why Vector Search Alone Isn't Enough

⚠️ You struggled here

Pure vector search finds things that sound similar. But similar isn't always useful.

HydraDB adds structured signals, behavioural feedback, and graph relationships on top of vectors.

Simple version: Vectors ask "what matches?" — HydraDB asks "what will actually help this person right now?"

🏢 Tenants & Sub-Tenants

✅ You got this
🏢 Tenant = a whole company. Sub-tenant = a department. Users = employees. Each company can never see another's data.
  • B2C: each user gets their own sub-tenant (personal space)
  • B2B: each organisation gets its own tenant with full isolation

🏷️ Metadata — Precise Control Over Recall

⚠️ You struggled here

Metadata lets you attach labels to memories — like project = "strawberry" or team = "engineering" — and use them as hard filters during recall.

  • Restrict recall to a specific user, team, or time range
  • Build predictable pipelines for compliance or auditing
💡 Use metadata when you know exactly what you want. Use semantic recall when you don't.
